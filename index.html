About Me

My career spans 20+ years across military intelligence, behavioral science research, and applied data science. I started as a U.S. Air Force intelligence officer supporting special operations and counterterrorism missions, then spent a decade as a professor and researcher studying judgment and decision making, culminating in a Ph.D. in Quantitative Psychology and a faculty role at the National Intelligence University.

That research-driven, evidence-first mindset now shows up in my work as an applied data scientist. I currently specialize in fraud detection and risk modeling at Thomson Reuters Special Services, turning complex, high-dimensional data into reliable, production-ready models that protect systems and surface actionable intelligence.

Based in Los Angeles, CA, I apply that mindset across machine learning, statistical modeling, and data engineering.
